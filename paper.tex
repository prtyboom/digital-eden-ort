\section{Introduction}
Modern physics posits a fundamental divide between the observer and the observed. Ontological Resolution Theory (ORT) removes this divide, postulating that reality is a computational process of "resolving" a fundamental superposition into localized facts.

In this framework:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Absolute} corresponds to a vacuum state $\varepsilon = 1$ (Maximum Entropy).
    \item \textbf{Physical Reality} corresponds to a deviation $\delta = 1 - \varepsilon$.
    \item \textbf{Gravity} is the thermodynamic pressure of the system trying to return to $\varepsilon = 1$.
\end{itemize}

\section{The Engine: Digital Eden}
To test this hypothesis, we developed an open-source simulation engine. The reality field $\varepsilon_i$ evolves according to a discrete update rule combining inertia (history) and signal (observation):
\begin{equation}
    \varepsilon_i(t+1) = I \cdot \varepsilon_i(t) + (1 - I) \cdot S_{collective}
\end{equation}
where $I \in [0, 1]$ is the Inertia coefficient.

\section{Experiment: The Gethsemane Scenario}
We modeled a high-inertia environment ($I=0.95$) populated by:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Crowd (NPCs):} $N=200$, Coherence $C=1$, Worldview $\varepsilon \approx 0.2$.
    \item \textbf{The Operator (Agent X):} $N=1$, Coherence $C=50$, Worldview $\varepsilon = 1.0$.
    \item \textbf{The Apostles:} $N=12$, Coherence $C=5$, Worldview $\varepsilon = 0.9$.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Objective:} To determine if the Operator can sustain a local region of $\varepsilon \approx 1$ against the overwhelming pressure of the Crowd.
