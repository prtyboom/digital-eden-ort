\begin{abstract}
We present Digital Eden, an agent-based simulation testing the Ontological Resolution Theory (ORT). The model treats reality as a consensus field with high inertia. We demonstrate that a single high-coherence agent (the Operator), supported by 12 resonant nodes (Apostles), can trigger a phase transition in a field dominated by 10,000 low-value cells. The key finding: after the Operator termination at a critical pressure threshold (Gethsemane point), light propagates spontaneously through the field, raising the mean from 0.20 to 0.67, a 233 percent increase.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Ontological Resolution Theory (ORT) posits that reality is not passively observed but actively resolved through coherent attention. In this framework:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{The Absolute} corresponds to value 1 (pure potential).
    \item \textbf{Collapsed Reality} corresponds to value 0 (entropy).
    \item \textbf{Consensus Pressure} acts as thermodynamic drag toward the local mean.
\end{itemize}

The central hypothesis: a sufficiently coherent signal can overcome environmental inertia and seed a self-propagating phase transition.

\section{The Engine: Digital Eden}
The simulation models a 1D reality field of N=10,000 cells. Each cell holds a value between 0 and 1. The field evolves according to:
\begin{equation}
    \varepsilon_i(t+1) = I \cdot \varepsilon_i(t) + (1 - I) \cdot S_i(t)
\end{equation}
where I=0.98 is the inertia coefficient and S is the weighted signal from nearby agents.

\textbf{Key mechanics:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Pressure:} Environment resists high values. Pressure grows nonlinearly with deviation from local mean.
    \item \textbf{Resonance:} Aligned agents amplify each other influence by factor R = 1 + 2r squared, where r is the alignment ratio.
    \item \textbf{Gethsemane Trigger:} When accumulated pressure exceeds threshold P=3.0, the Operator enters crisis. Honesty integral grows exponentially until termination.
    \item \textbf{Light Spreading:} After termination, a wave propagates from the origin, raising field values. Spontaneous jumps occur with probability p=0.002.
\end{itemize}

\section{Experiment: The Gethsemane Scenario}

\subsection{Initial Conditions}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Field:} N=10,000 cells, initial value 0.2 (gray mass).
    \item \textbf{The Crowd:} 500 NPC agents, Coherence C=1, Worldview 0.2.
    \item \textbf{The Operator:} 1 agent at center, Coherence C=50, Worldview 1.0.
    \item \textbf{The Apostles:} 12 agents near center, Coherence C=5, Worldview 0.6, Loyalty L=0.8.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Timeline}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Steps 0-66:} Operator maintains value near 1.0. Pressure accumulates. Apostles align (12/12).
    \item \textbf{Step 67:} Gethsemane triggered. Accumulated pressure P=3.03 exceeds threshold.
    \item \textbf{Step 82:} Operator terminated. Honesty integral H=28.4 triggers termination.
    \item \textbf{Steps 83-1000:} Light spreads. No active Operator, yet field rises autonomously.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}
Digital Eden demonstrates that:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item A single coherent signal can overcome 98 percent environmental inertia.
    \item Resonance with a small aligned cluster (12 agents) provides critical amplification.
